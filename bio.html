Biography for Naturinda Emmanuel


About Me

Naturinda Emmanuel is a graduate student at the Dalla Lana School of Public Health, University of Toronto. He is pursuing a Master’s in Health Informatics focusing on Intelligent Medicine and Electronic Medical Records. Emmanuel is a Board-Certified and Licensed Biomedical Scientist, Entrepreneurship Teacher, and Researcher. He has made significant contributions to the scientific community through his research publications on Diabetology, Hypoglycemia, Hypertension, Frailty Syndrome, and RNA Extraction methods among others. Emmanuel has trained over 500 early-stage entrepreneurs across the African continent to address different problems in their local communities and build financially sustainable ventures. He has secured and successfully managed entrepreneurship and research funding from principal granting institutions, such as the Resolution Project, Queen's University, and Edplus at Arizona State University. Emmanuel has actively contributed to various multi-lateral projects, cultivating an extensive network of individuals worldwide and building collaborations with impactful organizations such as Mastercard Foundation, Africa CDC, and Dunin-Deshpande Queen's Innovation Centre among others. He has traveled extensively and has been a panelist and facilitator at both local and international conferences. A thoughtful leader who is deeply committed to healthcare access and financial inclusion, Emmanuel has worked tirelessly to build initiatives that are collaborative and known for bold action and transformative impact. As a three-time recipient of the prestigious Mastercard Foundation scholarship, Emmanuel embodies the scholarship's mission to empower young African leaders to drive social and economic transformation. ''I envision a world where every household in the entire world can have access to quality medical facilities and care irrespective of its financial status, ethnicity or geographical location'' -Naturinda Emmanuel, Baobab Summit, Accra Ghana,2017. Professional Interests: I’m interested in combining my medical background, entrepreneurial and research experience to design clinical IT infrastructures for startups, health institutions, and regional health agencies, as well as advising government leaders, Nongovernmental organizations and the private sector on navigating the evolving digital health landscape. For the next decade of my career,I will be focused on exploring USA-Canada-Africa relations & how to get the most important “Stuff” to those who are hard to reach.
